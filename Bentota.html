Bentota : Turtle Hatcheries

Why Bentota?

Bentota, Sri Lanka is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. With its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque landscapes, it offers a wide range of water sports, including snorkeling, scuba diving, surfing, and jet skiing. Its serene atmosphere and charming culture make it an ideal destination for relaxation and rejuvenation. The town is also home to a variety of historic sites, temples, and nature reserves, providing a glimpse into Sri Lanka's rich heritage and biodiversity. Overall, Bentota promises a memorable and unforgettable travel experience

Turtle hatcheries in Bentota are a must-visit for nature enthusiasts and conservationists. These hatcheries provide a safe haven for turtle eggs and hatchlings, protecting them from predators and environmental hazards. Visitors can witness the release of baby turtles into the sea or learn about the conservation efforts and challenges faced by these endangered species. Hatcheries offer a unique opportunity to interact with these majestic creatures, understand their life cycle, and contribute to their preservation. It's a heartwarming and enlightening experience that leaves a lasting impact on visitors


Bentota Turtle Hatcheries

Visiting turtle hatcheries in Bentota is not just an enjoyable and educational experience, but also an opportunity to contribute to the conservation of endangered turtle species. By supporting these hatcheries, visitors can play an active role in protecting and preserving these majestic creatures, ensuring their survival for generations to come.

In turtle hatcheries in Bentota, visitors can witness baby turtles hatching and being released into the sea. They can also learn about the conservation efforts being made to protect these endangered species, and how they can support them. Some hatcheries also offer the opportunity to volunteer and help with the conservation work


Locations of Turtle Hatcheries in Bentota

There are several turtle hatcheries in Bentota, Sri Lanka. As turtles are an important part of Sri Lanka's biodiversity, these hatcheries play a crucial role in their conservation and protection. They also serve as popular tourist destinations, offering visitors a unique opportunity to learn about these fascinating creatures and their habitats, and to contribute to their preservation efforts
